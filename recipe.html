Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, and a blend of spices. Known for its vibrant red color and rich, smoky flavor, it's perfect for parties, Sunday dinners, or any time you want to enjoy a taste of home. This version is beginner-friendly but packed with flavor.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, blended
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 bay leaf

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot, then saut√© onions until translucent.
  3. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, stirring to avoid burning.
  4. Add blended tomatoes, bell peppers, and Scotch bonnet. Cook for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Stir in thyme, curry powder, bay leaf, and chicken stock. Bring to a boil.
  6. Add the rice, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Fluff with a fork, adjust seasoning if needed, and serve hot.

Tips

For a smokier flavor, cover the pot tightly and allow the rice to steam well. You can also add grilled chicken or fried plantains on the side for a complete meal.
